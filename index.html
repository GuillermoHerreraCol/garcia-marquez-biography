Gabriel García Márquez

Master of Magical Realism & Nobel Laureate

Colombian novelist, short-story writer, screenwriter and journalist, known for his magical realist novels and stories.

1927 - 2014

Life Timeline

Born in Aracataca

Gabriel José de la Concordia García Márquez was born in Aracataca, Colombia, a small town that would later inspire the fictional Macondo.

Started Journalism Career

Began working as a journalist while studying law at Universidad Nacional de Colombia.

First Novel Published

"Leaf Storm" (La Hojarasca) was published, marking the beginning of his literary career.

One Hundred Years of Solitude

His masterpiece was published, becoming one of the most important works of 20th-century literature.

Nobel Prize in Literature

Awarded the Nobel Prize "for his novels and short stories, in which the fantastic and the realistic are combined in a richly composed world of imagination."

Literary Legacy

García Márquez passed away in Mexico City, leaving behind an unprecedented literary legacy.

Major Works

One Hundred Years of Solitude

1967

The multi-generational story of the Buendía family and the mythical town of Macondo. A cornerstone of magical realism.

Love in the Time of Cholera

1985

A sweeping love story that spans over fifty years, exploring themes of love, aging, and death.

Chronicle of a Death Foretold

1981

A journalistic reconstruction of a murder that explores fate, honor, and collective responsibility.

The Autumn of the Patriarch

1975

A complex portrait of dictatorship and power, told through the story of an aging Latin American dictator.

Global Legacy

Birthplace: Colombia

Born in Aracataca, the inspiration for the fictional town of Macondo in his novels.

Home: Mexico

Lived in Mexico City for many years, where he wrote his most famous works and passed away in 2014.

Recognition: Sweden

Received the Nobel Prize in Literature in Stockholm in 1982 for his contribution to world literature.
